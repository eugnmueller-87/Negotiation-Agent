## What drives cost

This category is really four sub-markets bundled under one owner, and the cost lever differs in each. **Air** cost is driven by route/cabin mix and share-of-wallet, not a single "discount." **Hotel** cost is the negotiated corporate rate versus the open BAR (best available rate), plus whether last-room-availability (LRA) and inclusions (breakfast, wifi, parking) actually hold. **The TMC** (travel management company) is not where your spend sits — it's an intermediary whose fee model quietly decides whether it steers travellers toward *your* savings or *its* supplier incentives. **Catering / meetings & events (MICE)** cost is cost-per-head (or cost-per-cover) versus subsidy, plus minimum-spend and attrition/cancellation clauses. The biggest structural driver across all four is **leakage and compliance**: negotiated rates are worthless if travellers book off-programme, so adoption/channel-share is the true price lever. Money also hides in **soft-dollar incentives** (airline/hotel signing bonuses, free tickets, marketing funds) that flow to the buyer *organisation* but can distort category choices if not governed.

## The key levers

- **Hotel: negotiate LRA and dynamic-vs-fixed deliberately.** A fixed corporate rate is only real if it's LRA (available whenever a room is) — a non-LRA rate that vanishes on busy nights is a trap. A dynamic rate (a fixed % off BAR) protects you in soft markets but exposes you in peaks; a fixed rate does the reverse. Choose per property by demand pattern, and always pin the *inclusions*.
- **TMC: separate the fee model from the savings.** Push for transparent transaction-fee (per-booking) pricing or a management fee, and demand full disclosure of supplier rebates/overrides the TMC earns. A "free"/rebate-funded TMC is paid by your suppliers to route your travellers — a structural conflict. Insist on rebate transparency and, ideally, that overrides are passed back or netted.
- **Air: trade share-of-wallet for a corporate discount and soft-dollar terms.** Airline deals reward committed share on specific routes. Negotiate the point-of-sale/route-specific discount, waivers & favours, and lounge/status perks — and govern soft-dollar incentives as programme value, not a personal perk.
- **Catering: cost-per-head with a floor, not open subsidy.** Move from an open subsidy (you top up whatever it costs) to a capped cost-per-head with defined menu tiers. Attack food-cost pass-through, labour, and management fee separately as in facilities.
- **Meetings & events: unbundle, then re-bundle as a concession.** M&E and transient hotel spend are separate negotiations; bundle them only to *earn* a rate cut, and control attrition, cancellation, and minimum-spend clauses — these are where hotels recover margin.
- **Volume commitment / minimum spend as currency, not a gift.** Commit measurable share you'd spend anyway; keep any minimum-spend or attrition floor soft (a band, not a penalty cliff).
- **Rebate / volume tiers over headline discount.** When a supplier resists a rate cut, a spend-triggered rebate returns the same money and only pays if the volume lands.

## Category-specific traps suppliers use

- **Non-LRA "corporate rates"** quoted as a discount but unavailable exactly when you travel.
- **BAR-relative dynamic rates with no cap**, so the "discount" floats on a rate the hotel controls.
- **Rebate-funded / "free" TMC** whose supplier overrides steer travellers away from your best fares.
- **Undisclosed supplier overrides and GDS incentives** the TMC keeps.
- **Attrition & cancellation clauses** on M&E that bill you for unfilled room blocks / covers.
- **Minimum-spend cliffs** and **soft-dollar incentives** dangled to lock in share regardless of actual best value.
- **Inclusions stripped after signing** (breakfast/wifi/parking billed back).

## What to trade (give / get)

- **Give share-of-wallet / committed volume → get air discount + hotel rate + rebate tier.** Route committed share to the supplier's priority routes/properties — high value to them, spend you'd make anyway.
- **Give contract length → get a firmer rate and LRA guarantee.** Longer term lets hotels/airlines plan; extract the rate cut *and* a benchmarking/market-review right so you're not locked above market.
- **Give faster payment days → get price or rebate.** Fewer payment days is genuine cash-flow value to hotels and caterers; a clean low-weight give to pull down cost.
- **Give M&E/meeting bundling → get transient rate cut and fee reduction.** Grant the bundle only for a concession.
- **Take a rebate when a supplier won't cut the headline rate** — verifiable, contingent on realised volume.

## Engine mapping

- **price** = negotiated hotel rate (fixed or % off BAR), TMC transaction fee, air discount, catering cost-per-head/subsidy cap. Minimize; prioritise fee transparency and LRA over a headline number.
- **payment_days** = DPO; low-weight give, high supplier value — trade for price/rebate.
- **contract_length** = amortisation lever; concede only bundled with a benchmarking/market-review right.
- **volume** = committed share-of-wallet / room nights / covers / minimum spend; keep floors soft.
- **rebate** = spend/volume-triggered return and the vehicle for netting TMC overrides back to you.

## Key levers

- Demand LRA (last-room-availability) on every fixed corporate hotel rate — a non-LRA rate that disappears on busy nights is not a discount
- Choose fixed vs dynamic (% off BAR) rate per property by demand pattern, and cap any BAR-relative rate
- Force TMC fee-model transparency (per-transaction or management fee) and full disclosure of supplier overrides/rebates — net them back to you
- Trade committed air share-of-wallet for route-specific corporate discount, waivers & favours, and govern soft-dollar incentives as programme value not a personal perk
- Move catering from open subsidy to a capped cost-per-head with defined menu tiers; decompose food, labour and management fee
- Unbundle transient hotel and M&E spend, then grant bundling only in exchange for a rate/fee cut; control attrition and cancellation clauses
- Keep minimum-spend and attrition floors soft (bands, not penalty cliffs) and pin inclusions (breakfast/wifi/parking) in writing
- Take a spend-triggered rebate tier when a supplier resists a headline rate cut, and concede faster payment days as a low-weight give for price

## Sourcing discipline

This playbook contains NO invented figures, and a supplier-facing agent must not assert any of the following as fact — each must be sourced live at negotiation time and cited with a retrieval date: (1) any hotel corporate-rate discount %, the current BAR for a property, or a typical % off BAR — pull from live RFP/rate data (e.g. an RFP tool) for the specific property/date. (2) TMC transaction-fee levels, management-fee amounts, and the size of supplier overrides/GDS incentives — these vary by market and contract and must come from the actual TMC bid and disclosure, not an assumed 'brokers take X%'. (3) Airline corporate-discount percentages, route-specific fares, and soft-dollar/waiver values — from the airline's actual proposal and your own booking data. (4) Catering cost-per-head, food-cost %, and prevailing subsidy levels — from the specific caterer's bid and a live market benchmark. (5) Attrition/cancellation percentages, minimum-spend thresholds, and volume-rebate tier breakpoints — contract-specific, to be negotiated and sourced, never defaulted. (6) VAT treatment of travel, hotel, catering and MICE spend, and any EU/German statutory rules on entertainment/hospitality deductibility — must be confirmed against current tax guidance for the jurisdiction, not stated from memory. (7) Adoption/channel-share and leakage rates — must come from the buyer's own travel-programme data. Any share-of-wallet or committed-volume figure used as a lever must be the buyer's real spend, not an estimate.
