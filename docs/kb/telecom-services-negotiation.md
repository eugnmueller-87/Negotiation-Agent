## What drives cost

Telecom cost is **unit rate × consumed quantity + surcharge/overage creep + lifecycle lock-in** — and most of the leak is below the headline per-line price. The carrier concedes on the advertised per-line/per-GB rate and recovers it through **overage rates, out-of-bundle usage, roaming, premium/service numbers, and tariff sprawl** (dozens of legacy plans, each with its own rate, so nobody can benchmark). Cost also swings with **pooling** (a shared data pool across all lines almost always beats per-line allowances — heavy users borrow from light ones instead of every line paying overage), **technology-refresh timing** (a long fixed term locks you to today's tech and hardware while the market keeps cutting the effective rate), and **connectivity architecture** (buying capacity/circuits vs. metered usage). For postal/courier: **volume banding, weight/format steps, and surcharges** (fuel, remote-area, peak, residential, dimensional weight) do the same job accessorials do in freight. Award is typically a **framework/tender across a service matrix** (mobile, fixed data, voice, IoT/M2M SIMs, postal tiers) — you allocate a portfolio, not one number.

## The key levers

- **Consolidate and simplify the tariff first.** Collapse legacy plans into a small standard set before you price. Sprawl is the carrier's shield against benchmarking; a clean catalogue is your precondition for competition.
- **Pool data, don't allowance it per line.** Negotiate one shared pool with an aggregate overage rate, and cap or zero-rate overage inside the pool. Per-line allowances manufacture overage revenue.
- **Attack overage and out-of-bundle rates as their own line.** Cap the per-GB overage rate, bundle or flatten premium/service-number and international-direct-dial, and define what "unlimited" excludes (fair-use throttling).
- **Roaming: get EU regulated + a defined rest-of-world tariff.** Inside the EU, roam-like-at-home is the statutory baseline (verify current rules live); the negotiable prize is a capped or bundled **rest-of-world** roaming tariff and a hard bill-shock cap.
- **Decouple term from technology-refresh risk.** If you give a longer term, extract a mid-term tech-refresh/price-review right (benchmark-down clause) and a device-refresh path so the term doesn't freeze you at today's rate.
- **Keep MVNO / aggregator competition credible.** Carriers price against the threat that you move to an MVNO or a managed-mobility aggregator. Name it. It disciplines the incumbent's "we run the network" premium.
- **Buy SLA credits that bite.** Availability targets for circuits/connectivity with **service credits that are automatic and material** — not a token gesture you have to claim through a dispute process.
- **Protect exit: portability + early-termination.** Number portability guaranteed and fast, no clawback of past discounts on early exit, and staged (not cliff) termination fees. This is your BATNA insurance.
- **Postal/courier: band the volume, cap the surcharges.** Negotiate volume tiers, weight/format step boundaries, and fix/cap fuel and ancillary surcharges the same way you'd cap an FSC.

## Category-specific traps

- **Tariff sprawl** — dozens of legacy plans kept alive precisely so you can't benchmark or pool.
- **"Unlimited" with silent throttling / fair-use** that reintroduces overage economics.
- **Overage and premium-number rates left uncapped** while the per-line rate is cut to win the deal.
- **Auto-renewal / evergreen rollover** and discount clawback on early termination that trap you past the tech-refresh point.
- **Bundled "free" handsets** amortised into the line rate at a hidden financing markup — separate device cost from airtime.
- **Roaming and rest-of-world** left to standard tariff, and **bill-shock** with no spend cap.
- **Postal surcharges** (fuel, remote-area, dimensional weight, peak) undefined or "per published tariff" so they ratchet mid-term.

## What to trade (give / get)

- **Give contract length, get unit rate — but only with a tech-refresh/benchmark-down right.** A longer term is cheap to you *if* it can't freeze the rate; trade it for a lower per-line/per-GB rate plus a mid-term price review.
- **Give volume / line-count consolidation, get rate + a rebate tier.** Committing your whole estate (and IoT/M2M SIMs) to one carrier is high value to them; route it for a lower pooled rate and a volume-triggered rebate.
- **Give payment days, get price.** Faster payment is real cash-flow value; concede it (a low-weight term for most buyers) to pull down the overage cap or the rest-of-world roaming tariff — a clean logroll.
- **Take a rebate over a headline cut** when the carrier defends list price: a volume/spend-triggered rebate returns the same money, protects their reference price, and only pays if volume lands.
- **Hold the line on overage caps, SLA credits, roaming, and exit terms** — these are your high-weight terms; don't spend concessions there.

## Engine mapping

- **price** = per-line + per-GB + capped overage + roaming + postal band/surcharge rates (minimize); decompose it, never one all-in number.
- **contract length** = currency you concede for rate — but pair every extension with a tech-refresh/benchmark-down clause.
- **volume** = estate/line-count/SIM consolidation and postal volume bands, routed to win rate and a rebate tier.
- **payment days** = low-weight give, high supplier value; spend it to pull down overage/roaming.
- **rebate** = the face-saving substitute for a list-price cut, tied to committed volume/spend.


## Key levers

- Consolidate legacy tariff sprawl into a small standard catalogue before pricing, so plans can be benchmarked and competed
- Pool data across all lines with a capped aggregate overage rate instead of per-line allowances that manufacture overage revenue
- Cap the per-GB overage and out-of-bundle/premium-number rates as their own negotiated line, not just the headline per-line rate
- Secure EU roam-like-at-home baseline (verify live) plus a capped/bundled rest-of-world roaming tariff and a hard bill-shock cap
- Trade longer contract length for a lower unit rate ONLY with a mid-term tech-refresh and benchmark-down price-review right
- Keep MVNO / managed-mobility aggregator competition explicit to discipline the incumbent's network premium
- Buy availability SLAs with automatic, material service credits — not token credits claimable only through a dispute process
- Protect exit with guaranteed fast number portability, no discount clawback on early termination, and staged (not cliff) termination fees
- For postal/courier, band the volume and fix/cap fuel, remote-area, dimensional-weight and peak surcharges like a freight FSC
- Concede faster payment days (low buyer weight, high supplier value) to pull down overage caps and rest-of-world roaming rates

## Sourcing discipline

This playbook contains NO specific numbers, and every figure below must be sourced live before an agent asserts it — a supplier-facing agent must never state any of these as fact from memory: (1) EU roaming rules — "roam-like-at-home" and any regulated wholesale/retail caps or fair-use limits must be confirmed against the current EU Roaming Regulation in force at negotiation time, not assumed; (2) any per-line, per-GB, or overage benchmark rate must be benchmarked against live current market bids/indices for the region and volume; (3) typical/target overage caps, SLA availability percentages, and service-credit amounts must be pulled from current market norms and the carrier's published SLA, not invented; (4) rest-of-world roaming rates, premium/service-number rates, and bill-shock cap levels must be sourced from live tariffs; (5) postal/courier volume-band boundaries, weight/format step rates, and surcharge levels (fuel, remote-area, dimensional weight, peak) must be taken from the carrier's current published tariff and a live rate benchmark; (6) any MVNO/aggregator price delta vs. the incumbent, any rebate threshold or tier percentage, and any early-termination fee schedule must be sourced from actual current quotes. State all of these as "to be benchmarked" with the live source and retrieval date. Also unverified here and to confirm live if in scope: whether German/EU public-procurement thresholds or framework rules (e.g. VgV/GWB) constrain the award mechanism for this specific buyer.
