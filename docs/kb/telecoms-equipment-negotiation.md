## What drives cost

Telecoms & AV cost is almost never the box price you're quoted — it's the **total cost of the estate over its life**: hardware, install/commissioning, cabling and infrastructure, the maintenance/support attach, and the refresh you're quietly locked into. Suppliers and their VARs/integrators earn thin margin on hardware and fat, recurring margin on **maintenance, support contracts, licences, and the next refresh**. The headline "discount off list" is theatre — list price is a fiction the vendor controls, so a big percentage off an inflated list can still be expensive. What actually moves total cost: **standardisation** (one platform, fewer SKUs, volume leverage), **interoperability** (open standards vs proprietary lock-in that dictates who supplies the next phase), **the labour lines** (install, commissioning, cabling — often sole-sourced to the integrator), and the **support/SLA tier** you're steered into. In an EU/German context, framework agreements and public tenders (EU procurement / CPV-coded) shape leverage, and standards conformance (e.g. open AV-over-IP, SIP for telephony) is your best anti-lock-in weapon.

## The key levers

- **Price to net, not to discount-off-list.** Demand the net unit price and benchmark it; a percentage off a list the vendor sets is meaningless. Decompose every quote: hardware, install/commissioning, cabling/infrastructure, maintenance, warranty, spares — negotiate each line, never an all-in bundle you can't unpick.
- **Unbundle install/commissioning.** Bundled install hides labour margin and ties you to one integrator. Get it separately priced and, where possible, competed — or use it as a concession you *grant* for a hardware/maintenance cut, not a default.
- **Attack the maintenance/support attach.** This is the recurring profit centre. Push the SLA tier down to what you actually need, cap annual uplift, define response/fix times with teeth, and refuse maintenance that silently renews. Question whether year-1 warranty makes paid support redundant.
- **Standardise to build volume leverage.** Consolidating on fewer platforms/SKUs turns scattered spend into committed volume — your strongest price lever. Grant standardisation only in exchange for price/rebate.
- **Break interoperability lock-in.** Insist on open standards and documented interfaces so the *next* phase isn't sole-sourced to the incumbent. Proprietary codecs, closed control systems, and vendor-only cabling are lock-in traps priced as "integration."
- **Warranty, spares, RMA as real terms.** Extend warranty coverage, negotiate advance-replacement RMA and defined turnaround, and secure guaranteed spares availability / end-of-life notice so you're not forced into an early refresh.
- **Frame capex vs opex deliberately.** Outright purchase (capex) vs lease/DaaS/managed (opex) changes cash profile and who owns refresh risk. Opex models often bury a high effective interest rate and a refresh lock-in — make the financing cost explicit before choosing.

## Category-specific traps suppliers use

- **Inflated list price → headline discount** that still leaves you above market net.
- **Loss-leader hardware, recovered on maintenance/support** and the mandatory refresh.
- **Proprietary lock-in sold as "integration"** so phase 2 and the refresh are sole-sourced to them.
- **Bundled install/cabling** hiding labour margin and blocking competitive tender of the work.
- **Auto-renewing, uplift-uncapped maintenance** and SLAs with no real remedy for breach.
- **Early end-of-life / end-of-support** engineered to force a premature (full-price) refresh.
- **Opex/DaaS quotes** that hide the financing rate and lock you into the vendor's refresh cycle.

## What to trade (give / get)

- **Give contract length → get price + capped maintenance.** A longer term lets the vendor amortise install and plan; extract a real net-price cut, a maintenance uplift cap, and a mid-term benchmarking/break right. Never give length without the exit and price protection.
- **Give committed volume / standardisation → get rebate + lower net.** Consolidating SKUs is high value to the vendor and something you'd do anyway — convert it into tiered pricing or a retro rebate, not just a one-off discount.
- **Give faster payment days → get price.** Improved cash flow has genuine value to a hardware-heavy supplier and their distributor; a low-weight give for you, trade it for net price or warranty extension.
- **Give bundled install (as a granted concession) → get hardware/maintenance cut** — only when unbundling can't be competed and you extract value for it.
- **Take rebate over headline discount** when the vendor resists a net-price cut — a volume-triggered rebate returns the same money and only pays if volume lands.

## Engine mapping

- **price** = the *net* unit price plus the maintenance/support line and install — prioritise net and recurring cost over discount-off-list.
- **payment_days** = DPO; a low-weight give to trade for price or warranty.
- **contract_length** = the amortisation/lock-in lever — concede only with a benchmarking/break right and capped maintenance uplift.
- **volume** = standardised/committed estate — convert to rebate and lower net, not just discount.
- **rebate** = the retro mechanism where volume commitment and standardisation are returned as cash.

## Sourcing discipline

This playbook contains NO benchmark numbers, and none may be invented at negotiation time. Every figure a buyer would want must be sourced live and cited with a retrieval date; a supplier-facing agent must state these as "to be benchmarked", never assert them as fact:
- Net market price / typical discount-off-list for the specific hardware (switchboards/PABX, handsets, AV displays, codecs, cabling, routers) — benchmark against live distributor pricing, recent bids, or the buyer's own spend data; the list price itself is vendor-set and must not be treated as a market anchor.
- Maintenance/support attach rate as a % of hardware, typical SLA response/fix times per tier, and standard annual uplift/indexation caps — source from the actual quotes and a live market benchmark.
- Install/commissioning and cabling labour rates (per point/per day/per m) — get current market/integrator rates for the region.
- Warranty durations, RMA/advance-replacement turnaround norms, and spares/end-of-life notice periods — confirm from the vendor's published terms and market norm at negotiation time.
- The effective financing/interest rate embedded in any lease/DaaS/opex quote — require the vendor to disclose it; do not estimate it.
- Volume-rebate thresholds and tier percentages — set from the buyer's real committed volume, not a made-up figure.
- EU/German procurement specifics (framework-agreement mechanics, EU procurement thresholds, CPV coding, applicable standards conformance) are real but must be confirmed against the current statutory/tender source for the specific contract, with date, rather than asserted from memory.
