## What drives cost in this category

Agency cost is not one number — it's a stack, and most of it hides below the fee you're quoted. The four cost pools:

1. **Agency labour/fee** — retainer, project fee, FTE cost-plus, or commission. This is the visible line.
2. **Media margin** — where the real money leaks. On principal/proprietary media the agency buys inventory itself and resells it to you at an opaque markup; on "gross" buying you pay rate-card and the agency keeps the difference. **AVBs/rebates** (agency volume bonuses paid by media owners) may never reach you unless the contract says they must.
3. **Rights & usage** — talent, music, and asset licences that expire. Under-scoped buyouts create forced re-negotiations later at zero leverage.
4. **Scope creep** — out-of-SOW work billed at ad-hoc rates, and "included" hours that quietly inflate the retainer.

The category's defining trap: the supplier steers you to negotiate the fee (small, visible) while protecting media margin and rebates (large, invisible).

## The key levers

- **Decouple media from creative.** Separate SOWs/contracts for media buying vs creative removes the bundle that lets an agency subsidise a cheap fee with hidden media margin. Insist on a media-neutral, auditable buying arrangement.
- **Media transparency clause.** Contract for **net (disclosed) buying**, full pass-through of all AVBs/rebates/free space, principal-media disclosure, and an **independent audit right** with a defined look-back window. No audit right = no transparency.
- **Fee-model choice is a lever, not a given.** Retainer (predictable, prone to over-staffing), project/SOW (disciplined, admin-heavy), FTE cost-plus (transparent labour, cap the multiplier), or performance/incentive (aligns interest, needs clean KPIs). Push cost-plus + capped margin where you can see the inputs; use performance to claw back fixed fee.
- **Rate card + blended rate.** Attack the seniority mix, not just hourly rates — agencies pad plans with senior time. Negotiate a **blended rate** tied to an agreed pyramid, cap principal/EVP hours, and require timesheet substantiation.
- **SOW discipline.** Every engagement gets a written scope, deliverables, and a change-control mechanism. Kill "T&M for anything not listed."
- **Rights/usage buyouts.** Negotiate usage term, territory, and renewal cost **up front** in the original SOW — never after the asset is live.
- **Pitch leverage.** Competitive pitch or credible incumbent-review threat is your single biggest price mover; roster consolidation (volume) is the second.

## Category-specific traps suppliers use

- Cheap headline fee, recovered through media margin and unshared rebates.
- "Blended rate" quoted on a senior-heavy pyramid you never agreed.
- Retainer hours "used up" early to justify out-of-scope billing.
- Narrow usage rights that force a paid renewal when the campaign performs.
- Rebate/AVB language that's silent or defers to "market practice" — meaning the agency keeps it.

## What to trade (give low-value to get high-value)

- **Give longer contract length; get fee reduction + locked rate card + guaranteed rebate pass-through.** Agencies value tenure certainty highly; you weight fee and transparency higher.
- **Give volume/roster consolidation; get blended-rate cut and better usage terms.** Consolidated spend is cheap for you to promise and highly valued by them.
- **Give faster payment days; get margin/fee concession.** Cash flow is often the supplier's top ask — trade a term you weight lightly for one you weight heavily.
- **Hold the line on transparency and audit rights** — these are near-zero cost to you and directly attack the hidden cost pool. Never trade them away for a headline discount.

## Mapping to the engine terms

- **price** → agency fee level, blended rate, and disclosed media margin/markup.
- **payment_days** → billing terms; a cheap give for you, a strong get for the agency (cash flow).
- **contract_months** → tenure; trade length for fee and transparency commitments.
- **volume_units** → consolidated/committed spend or roster share; concede to unlock rate cuts.
- **rebate_pct** → the AVB/rebate pass-through and any performance/incentive claw-back — the category's highest-leverage hidden term. Weight it heavily; it is where category cost actually hides.

## Key levers

- Decouple media from creative into separate SOWs so a cheap fee can't be subsidised by hidden media margin
- Contract for net/disclosed media buying with 100% AVB/rebate pass-through and an independent audit right + look-back window
- Choose the fee model as a lever: prefer cost-plus with a capped multiplier or performance-linked over open-ended retainers
- Negotiate the blended rate against an agreed seniority pyramid and cap principal/EVP hours, not just headline hourly rates
- Enforce SOW discipline with written scope + change control; eliminate open T&M for unlisted work
- Lock rights/usage term, territory, and renewal cost up front in the original SOW, never after the asset is live
- Use a competitive pitch or credible incumbent review as the primary price mover; consolidate the roster for volume leverage
- Trade contract length and faster payment for fee cuts, locked rate cards, and guaranteed rebate pass-through

## Sourcing discipline

This playbook deliberately states NO specific numbers. Any figure a real negotiation needs must be sourced live and never asserted by the agent: (1) typical agency markup / media margin percentages — benchmark against the current market and the specific agency's disclosed inputs; (2) prevailing AVB/rebate levels by market and media type — these vary by country, media owner, and regulatory regime and must be pulled from current data or an audit, not assumed; (3) blended-rate and hourly-rate benchmarks by role/seniority and geography — source from a current rate-card benchmark; (4) standard payment-term norms and typical retainer sizing for the client's region/sector. The agent must present these as "benchmark this live" rather than fabricate. Also: the existence and enforceability of rebate pass-through and audit rights is jurisdiction-dependent (e.g. differing disclosure norms across markets) — verify against the governing law and any applicable industry transparency guidance before asserting a contractual entitlement.
