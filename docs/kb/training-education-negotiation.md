## What drives cost in this category

Training cost is a stack, not a day rate. The pools:

1. **Delivery labour** — instructor-led day rate (per trainer per day) or e-learning platform PEPM (per-enrolled-user-per-month / per-seat licence). This is the visible line and where suppliers steer the haggle.
2. **Content development** — bespoke course build, often quoted once but the vendor keeps the IP and re-sells the same content to your competitors. You paid to build it and cannot reuse it.
3. **Pricing-unit arbitrage** — per-seat, per-cohort, and licence models price the *same* delivery very differently. A per-seat quote punishes you for filling the room; per-cohort rewards it. Vendors quote the unit that maximises their take against your expected fill.
4. **Cancellation / rescheduling and materials** — late-cancel and reschedule fees, printed/licensed materials, LMS hosting, and travel/expenses stacked on the instructor rate.

The defining trap: the supplier makes you negotiate the headline day rate or PEPM while protecting content ownership, the pricing unit, and cancellation terms — where the real money and lock-in live.

## The key levers (concrete)

- **Force the right pricing unit.** For high-fill internal cohorts, buy **per-cohort/per-day** (fixed cost, you keep the seat-fill upside) not per-seat. For sparse, long-tail audiences, per-seat or PEPM shifts risk to the vendor. Make them quote all three and pick the one that fits *your* utilisation — never accept the unit they lead with.
- **Attack PEPM on the active-user definition.** E-learning PEPM is billed on *enrolled* or *provisioned* seats, not *active* learners. Negotiate billing on active/consumed seats, a true-down (not just true-up) clause, and no charge for dormant licences. This is often the single biggest e-learning saving.
- **Content ownership / reuse rights.** Decide up front: work-for-hire (you own the bespoke content you funded), a perpetual internal-reuse licence, or a discount because they retain resale rights. Never pay full bespoke-build price *and* leave IP with the vendor — that is paying twice.
- **Train-the-trainer to internalise.** Buy the right to certify your own trainers so recurring delivery moves in-house. This caps your dependence and is your strongest long-run price lever — pay a one-off certification fee to escape a perpetual day-rate.
- **Cancellation / rescheduling terms.** Push the fee cliff back (e.g. free reschedule outside a defined window) and make cancellation a sliding scale, not a flat penalty. Vendors default to aggressive cliffs because unfilled trainer days are their risk — make them share it.
- **Outcome / completion-based pricing.** Where measurable (certification pass rate, completion %), tie a slice of fee to outcomes to claw back spend on no-shows and non-completers. Needs a clean, pre-agreed KPI or it becomes a dispute.
- **Materials.** Unbundle printed/licensed materials from the day rate; prefer digital/reusable, and get per-learner material cost itemised so it is not a hidden per-head tax.
- **Volume commitment vs flexibility.** Committed seats/cohorts unlock the best unit price but strand you if demand drops. Trade a realistic floor commitment for the rate, and keep a flex band above it priced at the same unit — don't over-commit to a forecast.

## Category-specific traps suppliers use

- **Per-seat quote on a room you'll fill anyway** — you pay for utilisation you were always going to deliver.
- **PEPM on provisioned, not active, seats** — you fund licences nobody logs into.
- **Bespoke build billed to you, IP retained by vendor** — you paid R&D; they resell it.
- **Aggressive cancellation cliffs** framed as "standard terms" — they are highly negotiable.
- **"Included" materials or LMS hosting** that quietly inflate the per-head or PEPM line.
- **Content refresh / update fees** that turn a one-time build into an annuity.

## What to trade (give low-value to get high-value)

- **Give longer contract length + committed volume → get a lower unit rate, a broad internal-reuse licence, and train-the-trainer rights.** Vendors weight utilisation certainty highly; you weight unit price and lock-in escape higher.
- **Give faster payment (shorter payment days) → get a rate or PEPM concession.** Cash flow is a common supplier ask and cheap for you to concede if cash allows.
- **Give a realistic floor volume commitment → get a bigger volume rebate and softer cancellation terms.**
- **Hold the line on content ownership, active-user billing, and audit of completion data** — near-zero cost to you, and they attack the hidden cost and lock-in directly. Never trade them for a headline day-rate discount.

## Mapping to the engine terms

- **price** (MINIMIZE) → the day rate, per-cohort fee, per-seat price, or PEPM — attack the *unit* and its definition, not just the headline number.
- **payment_days** (MAXIMIZE for buyer) → billing terms; a cheap give for you, a strong get for the vendor. Model as a give-term with high supplier appetite.
- **contract_length** → tenure; trade length for unit-price cuts, reuse rights, and train-the-trainer.
- **volume** → committed seats/cohorts/spend that unlocks the best unit price and rebate bands.
- **rebate_pct** (MAXIMIZE) → retrospective volume rebate on committed training spend; a get-term won via term + volume.
- Content ownership/reuse rights, train-the-trainer certification, cancellation-schedule softness, and outcome-based fee-at-risk are category terms outside the core five — carry them as explicit logrolling give/get terms (typically high buyer weight on ownership and active-user billing).

## Key levers

- Make the vendor quote per-seat, per-cohort, and licence/PEPM, then pick the unit that fits your actual fill — never accept the one they lead with
- Bill e-learning PEPM on active/consumed seats with a true-down clause, not on provisioned or enrolled licences
- Settle content ownership up front: work-for-hire or a perpetual internal-reuse licence, never full bespoke-build price with IP left at the vendor
- Buy train-the-trainer certification to internalise recurring delivery and escape a perpetual day rate
- Turn cancellation/reschedule cliffs into a sliding scale with a free-reschedule window
- Tie a fee slice to completion/certification outcomes where a clean KPI exists, to claw back no-show and non-completion spend
- Unbundle and itemise materials and LMS hosting so they aren't a hidden per-head tax
- Trade contract length + a realistic floor volume for a lower unit rate, reuse rights, a bigger rebate, and softer cancellation terms; concede payment days for a rate cut

## Sourcing discipline

This playbook asserts NO specific numbers, and the agent must not either. Every figure a real negotiation needs must be sourced live and cited, never fabricated in a supplier-facing message: (1) prevailing instructor-led day-rate and PEPM/per-seat benchmarks by topic, seniority, language, and region — pull from a current rate benchmark, do not assume; (2) typical cancellation/reschedule fee windows and percentages — these are vendor- and contract-specific, quote from the actual terms sheet not from memory; (3) standard content-development cost and any refresh/update fee levels — get the vendor's itemised build cost, do not estimate; (4) train-the-trainer certification fee and any per-trainer recurring licence; (5) payment-term norms for the client's region/sector; (6) volume-rebate band thresholds. Legal/tax points to verify before asserting, not assume: German/EU VAT treatment of training services (some vocational/further-education training can be VAT-exempt under UStG §4 / the EU VAT Directive — this is fact-specific and must be confirmed with tax/legal, never stated as settled); whether public funding or co-financing rules apply (e.g. Bildungsgutschein / AZAV-certified provision, ESF-funded programmes) which can constrain pricing and eligibility; and the enforceability of IP/work-for-hire and completion-data audit clauses under the governing law. Present all of these as "benchmark/verify this live," never as a fixed figure.
